WHO WE ARE

“No one should be left out of this conversation.”
-Eboni Cochran

The human health risks associated with the chemicals released in West Louisville, an area populated mostly by Black residents and other people of color, are over 10,000 times higher than the industry average. That’s why residents in Southwest Louisville are over three times more likely to contract cancer than someone living in East Louisville.

Naming the issue is important, but we also want to change it. Air Justice doesn’t have all the answers yet, but we believe that good things come when communities band together. We aim to work with local communities to research solutions that decrease emissions and bring legislative change.

Air Justice is a project of the Environmental Health Literacy Coalition, a local group of community leaders, grassroots activists, and researchers who want to make the science of air pollution in Louisville easier to understand.

The reason it’s easy to ignore air pollution is because toxins are mostly invisible and the issue is buried under scientific and legal jargon. Residents should not have to sift through jargon to advocate for themselves. That’s why we want to make engaging with the problem of air pollution simpler and easier.

MEET THE TEAM

  • Councilwoman
    Keisha Dorsey

    Public Health & Local Legislation

    Councilwoman
    Keisha Dorsey

    is a native Louisvillian who studied biology at UofL before earning a Master's degree in Public Health. She was elected as District 3 councilwoman in 2018, and was voted the city's Most Admired Woman in Public Service in 2022. Since taking office, she has pushed for change in issues related to environmental justice. In 2020, she founded the Environmental Health Literacy Coalition, which created the Air Justice campaign in 2022.

    is a grassroots organization of residents living near or at the fence lines of a cluster of 11 chemical plants in Louisville commonly referred to as Rubbertown. REACT has worked consistently with Councilwoman Dorsey since she was elected and helped to form the Environmental Health Literacy Coalition.

OUR PRINCIPLES

Empowerment

Our coalition is rooted in feminist empowerment. We believe in equity, equality, and empowerment.

Everyone should have the right to choose not just how to live their life, but also choose what the future of their community looks like. But part of being empowered to make change in our communities comes from being able to participate in the conversations about what affects us and how.

Transparency

We believe in trust and transparency, between individual residents and between residents and their local government.

A lot of information about air pollution in Louisville is available to local residents, but making information available is not the same as making that information accessible. Scientific and legal information is only transparent if it is written to the local communities affected by that information.

Access

We believe that everyone should have access to a happy, healthy life in Louisville.

Everyone deserves to live a life free from toxic air. Regardless of what businesses insist on operating in our neighborhoods, we deserve clean air. And because our air has been polluted for far too long, we deserve equitable access to affordable healthcare that cares for families.
